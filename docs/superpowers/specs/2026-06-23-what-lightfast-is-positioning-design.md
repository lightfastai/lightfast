# What Lightfast Is - Positioning Design

Date: 2026-06-23
Status: Working draft for voice tuning
Area: company positioning, public site reset, research agenda, content strategy

## Purpose

This document captures the new direction for Lightfast so the older public
content, metadata, Open Graph text, README language, brand language, and blog
positioning can be replaced deliberately.

The previous public center of gravity was "the operating layer between agents
and apps." That language no longer matches the ambition. Lightfast is moving
toward a broader human-AI collaboration lab posture, with product and model work
organized around new mediums for complex work.

## Core Positioning

Lightfast is a human-AI collaboration lab building new mediums for complex work.

The company should be introduced as a research and product company first, not as
a SaaS product or agent infrastructure company. Products are evidence of the
research agenda, and research is shaped by what real products reveal.

## Primary Thesis

Most important tools were designed before AI could participate in the work
itself. They assume human-speed iteration, mostly linear artifacts, shallow
branching, external review loops, and AI as an add-on.

AI progress changes the medium of work, not just the speed of work. Lightfast is
building models, interfaces, environments, and collaboration systems together so
people and AI can work live inside complex technical and physical-world
projects.

## Positioning Decisions

- Lightfast is an AI lab/company first, product company second.
- The object of study is human-AI collaboration itself.
- The main noun is "new mediums," not just tools, apps, or infrastructure.
- The domain umbrella is "complex work," at least for now.
- The initial public examples should lean engineering, product, and physical
  world rather than generic creative work.
- Real-time collaboration is a core pillar, not a feature.
- AI should participate while the work is happening: live, multiplayer,
  branching, interruptible, and fast enough to keep up.
- Lightfast will do model work, but the frame is collaboration-native model
  work, not generic frontier-model chasing.
- Lightfast should speak about models, interfaces, environments, and
  collaboration systems as a unified design problem.
- The first wedge is software and product design because iteration loops are
  short and the company can learn fastest there.
- The broader ambition extends to architecture, construction, manufacturing,
  vehicles, research labs, infrastructure, operations, and physical-world
  production.
- The public site should lead with a company manifesto, then a research agenda,
  then product evidence.

## Internal North Star

The private north star is that this research should eventually help a small team
build things that currently require large institutions.

In founder terms: one day, Jeevan Pillay wants to be able to build a space
station.

Do not publish that exact line on the homepage or company page yet. Keep it in
the spec as scale and direction. Public copy can gesture toward buildings,
vehicles, factories, spacecraft, and infrastructure without making "space
station" the first public claim.

## Enemy

The enemy is AI bolted onto pre-AI tools.

Lightfast should avoid dunking on specific companies or tools. The critique is
structural: many excellent tools were built around older assumptions about how
work happens. AI as a sidebar, autocomplete box, command palette, or export step
does not fully change the medium.

Useful contrast:

> Most AI features sit beside the work. Lightfast is building mediums where AI
> participates in the work itself.

## Terms To Avoid Or De-Emphasize

- "Operating layer for agents and apps" - old direction.
- "Agent infrastructure" - too narrow and too associated with the prior product.
- "Workspace memory" - too small as a company-level thesis.
- "Objectives, decisions, tool use, evaluation" - may remain useful primitives,
  but should not be the top-level story.
- "Shared understanding" - useful but too soft and too narrow.
- "Coordination" - close, but still not expansive enough on its own.
- "Technical and creative work" - misses physical-world engineering and
  production.
- "Collaborative making" - too casual and craft-coded.
- "Speed of thought" - too cliche and too Neuralink-coded.
- Generic "frontier model lab" language - model work should be tied to
  collaboration-native systems.

## Preferred Phrases

- Human-AI collaboration lab.
- New mediums for complex work.
- Collaboration-native models and environments.
- AI should participate while the work is happening.
- Live, multiplayer, branching, interruptible, and fast enough to keep up.
- Models, interfaces, and environments designed together.
- Built from first principles for people and AI working together.
- Complex work that does not fit inside a prompt.

## Public Examples

Lead with domains like:

- software and product design;
- architecture and construction;
- manufacturing and production;
- vehicles and spacecraft;
- scientific research;
- infrastructure and operations.

Do not lead with music, photography, or film yet. They may matter later, but the
first signal should be engineering, product, and physical-world complexity.

## First Wedge

Start with software and product design.

This is where iteration loops are shortest, digital artifacts can be changed
quickly, and the boundaries between idea, design, code, and deployment are
already collapsing. It lets Lightfast prototype the collaboration model before
expanding toward harder physical-world domains.

The possible product metaphor "live workbench" is provisionally acceptable but
not final. It should not harden into brand language until the voice and product
shape are clearer.

## Content Implications

Future content work should update or replace:

- global site metadata and Open Graph copy;
- manifest text;
- `llms.txt` description;
- README and SPEC mission/vision language;
- brand guideline company description;
- company sheet copy;
- stale blog/category language around agent infrastructure;
- any "operating layer" and "company as a Program" public framing that no longer
  fits.

The first public site should not over-explain product taxonomy. It should make
the worldview legible and give enough concrete evidence to show the company is
serious.

## Voice Notes For Fine Tuning

The voice should be ambitious but grounded. It should sound like a lab with
taste and conviction, not a generic AI startup.

Do:

- speak structurally about the shift in tools;
- keep the critique of old tools respectful;
- use concrete domains and examples;
- keep real-time collaboration central;
- make model work feel necessary to the medium;
- write in clear, direct sentences.

Do not:

- imitate Thinking Machines Lab too closely;
- sound like science fiction;
- overuse "AI agents";
- claim frontier-model capability before there is evidence;
- collapse the ambition back into memory, objectives, decisions, or workflow
  software.
