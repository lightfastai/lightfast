# What Lightfast Is - Positioning Design

Date: 2026-06-23
Status: Approved direction for homepage voice and positioning reset
Area: company positioning, public site reset, research agenda, content strategy

## Purpose

This document captures the current direction for Lightfast so older public
content, metadata, Open Graph text, README language, brand language, and blog
positioning can be replaced deliberately.

The previous public center of gravity was "the operating layer between agents
and apps." That language no longer matches the ambition. The intermediate
phrasing, "a human-AI collaboration lab building new mediums for complex work,"
also asks the reader to decode too much. Lightfast should be introduced more
plainly as an applied AI lab building systems where teams and AI work together
in real time.

## Core Positioning

Public title:

> Teams and AI, Working Together in Real Time

Primary description:

> Lightfast is an applied AI lab building systems where product and engineering
> teams design, build, and ship with AI in real time.

Support line:

> We develop the primitives that make this possible: models, interfaces,
> infrastructure, and evals tested in real products.

The company should be introduced as an applied AI lab, not as a collaboration
lab, SaaS product, or agent infrastructure company. Products are evidence of the
research agenda, and research is shaped by what real products reveal.

## Primary Thesis

Most AI tools still sit outside the work. They appear as sidebars, prompt boxes,
autocomplete, command palettes, summaries, and export steps around tools that
were designed before models could participate in the work itself.

Lightfast is building systems where AI can participate while the work is
happening. For product and engineering teams, that means systems that can follow
changing context, act on shared information, help explore and compare
directions, manipulate artifacts, take feedback, and respond while decisions are
being made.

Real-time does not mean "chat responds quickly." It means the system is inside
the team's active work: close enough to the artifact, context, constraints,
conversation, and decision loop to be useful while the work is still changing.

## Positioning Decisions

- Lightfast is an applied AI lab first. It builds products as experiments and
  turns what works into reusable primitives.
- The object of study is real-time collaboration between teams and AI.
- The public homepage should use "teams and AI working together" more often than
  the academic category "human-AI collaboration."
- "Human-AI collaboration" is still useful as a field label for titles,
  metadata, research pages, and technical writing.
- The first public audience is product and engineering teams.
- The broader audience is engineering and design teams, including teams that
  work on software, products, machines, buildings, infrastructure, factories,
  and physical systems.
- The first wedge is software and product design because iteration loops are
  short and the company can learn fastest there.
- The broader ambition extends to architecture, construction, manufacturing,
  vehicles, research labs, infrastructure, operations, and physical-world
  production.
- Real-time collaboration is a core pillar, not a feature.
- AI should participate while the work is happening: live, multiplayer,
  branching, interruptible, and fast enough to keep up.
- "Primitives" is public language when attached to concrete buckets: models,
  interfaces, infrastructure, and evals.
- Lightfast may do model-level work, but the frame should be applied AI
  primitives for real products, not generic frontier-model chasing.
- The public site should behave more like a company/product page with manifesto
  pressure underneath, not a long essay as the first experience.

## Internal North Star

The private north star is that this research should eventually help a small team
build things that currently require large institutions.

In founder terms: one day, Jeevan Pillay wants to be able to build a space
station.

Do not publish that exact line on the homepage or company page yet. Keep it in
the spec as scale and direction. Public copy can gesture toward buildings,
vehicles, factories, spacecraft, and infrastructure without making "space
station" the first public claim.

## Enemy

The enemy is AI that sits outside the work.

Lightfast should avoid dunking on specific companies or tools. The critique is
structural: many excellent tools were built around older assumptions about how
teams design, build, review, decide, and ship. AI as a sidebar, autocomplete
box, command palette, or export step can be useful, but it does not fully solve
the collaboration problem.

Useful contrast:

> Most AI tools still sit outside the work. Lightfast builds systems where AI
> can participate while the work is happening.

## Terms To Avoid Or De-Emphasize

- "Operating layer for agents and apps" - old direction.
- "Agent infrastructure" - too narrow and too associated with the prior product.
- "Workspace memory" - too small as a company-level thesis.
- "Objectives, decisions, tool use, evaluation" - may remain useful primitives,
  but should not be the top-level story.
- "Human-AI collaboration lab" - makes collaboration sound like the company type
  instead of the applied AI problem space.
- "New mediums" - too abstract for the homepage and first sentence.
- "Complex work" - points at process instead of teams building ambitious things.
- "AI-native" and "collaboration-native" - useful internally at times, but too
  jargon-heavy as public headline language.
- "Shared understanding" - useful but too soft and too narrow.
- "Active working loop" - accurate internally, but too systems-theory-ish for
  public copy.
- "Coordination" - close, but not expansive enough on its own.
- "Technical and creative work" - misses physical-world engineering and
  production.
- "Collaborative making" - too casual and craft-coded.
- "Speed of thought" - too cliche and too Neuralink-coded.
- Generic "frontier model lab" language - model work should be tied to applied
  collaboration primitives tested in real products.
- "AI teammates," "digital coworkers," and similar phrases that over-personify
  the system.

## Preferred Phrases

- Applied AI lab.
- Teams and AI, working together in real time.
- Systems where product and engineering teams design, build, and ship with AI in
  real time.
- Primitives for real-time collaboration between teams and AI.
- Models, interfaces, infrastructure, and evals tested in real products.
- AI should participate while the work is happening.
- Most AI tools still sit outside the work.
- Products as experiments.
- Research through products.
- Built for teams first.
- Software and product design is the first wedge.

## Voice Rules

- Use "we" for conviction, not filler.
- Keep homepage copy spare and declarative. Save the exploratory essay voice for
  posts, research notes, and founder letters.
- Use concrete verbs near the top: design, build, ship, explore, edit, compare,
  review, decide, implement, deploy.
- Use "AI" for the team-facing experience and "models" for the technical layer.
- Avoid overusing "AI" in titles and headings when the surrounding sentence can
  carry the meaning.
- Bind research to products every time: Lightfast is not a think tank and not a
  pure model lab.
- Make "lab" operational: build real products, learn from usage, publish or
  expose what is learned, then turn that into reusable primitives.
- Keep the critique of old tools respectful and structural.
- Avoid claims about AGI on the homepage. Let the ambition shape the direction
  without pulling the reader into a model-race frame too early.

## Public Examples

Lead with domains like:

- software and product design;
- architecture and construction;
- manufacturing and production;
- vehicles and spacecraft;
- scientific research;
- infrastructure and operations.

Do not lead with music, photography, or film yet. They may matter later, but the
first signal should be product, engineering, and physical-world systems.

## First Wedge

Start with software and product design.

This is where iteration loops are shortest, digital artifacts can be changed
quickly, and the boundaries between idea, design, code, review, and deployment
are already collapsing. It lets Lightfast test primitives directly in real
products before expanding toward harder physical-world domains.

Avoid hardening a product metaphor like "workspace," "workbench," or "medium"
into brand language until the product shape is clearer. Use "systems" publicly
and reserve more specific nouns for actual product surfaces.

## Content Implications

Future content work should update or replace:

- global site metadata and Open Graph copy;
- manifest text;
- `llms.txt` description;
- README and SPEC mission/vision language;
- brand guideline company description;
- company sheet copy;
- stale blog/category language around agent infrastructure;
- any "operating layer" and "company as a Program" public framing that no longer
  fits.

The refreshed public site should not over-explain product taxonomy. It should
make the worldview legible quickly, prove that Lightfast is an applied AI lab,
and give enough concrete evidence to show the company is serious.

## Resolved Questions

- Replace "complex work" with team and domain language.
- Replace "new mediums" with systems, primitives, models, interfaces,
  infrastructure, and evals.
- Use "applied AI lab," not "collaboration lab."
- Use "Teams and AI, Working Together in Real Time" as the homepage title.
- Use product and engineering teams on the first screen.
- Keep "human-AI collaboration" as field/research language.
- Keep "primitives" public when attached to concrete buckets.
- Keep AGI ambition off the homepage for now.
