Lightfast is a human-AI collaboration lab building new mediums for complex work.

AI is changing what people can make, but most of the tools we use to make things were designed for a different era. Files, canvases, dashboards, timelines, tickets, branches, meetings, and review loops were built around human-speed iteration. AI is now fast enough to participate inside the work itself, but most tools still treat it as an add-on: a sidebar, a command palette, an autocomplete box, or an export step.

We think this misses the deeper shift.

The future of AI will not only be better models. It will be new mediums: environments where people and AI work together live, across many collaborators, many versions, many constraints, and many possible futures at once. Lightfast exists to build those mediums from first principles.

## AI Should Participate While The Work Is Happening

The most important work does not fit inside a prompt.

Designing a product, planning a building, engineering a vehicle, operating a construction site, debugging a production system, coordinating a manufacturing process, or exploring a scientific idea all require context, judgment, taste, constraints, collaboration, and constant revision. The work changes as people discuss it. The right answer depends on what just happened, what has been tried before, who is responsible, what constraints matter, and what the team is trying to become.

Current AI systems are powerful in fragments. They can answer questions, generate drafts, write code, summarize documents, and produce options. But real work is live. It happens between people, tools, materials, and decisions. It branches. It gets interrupted. It depends on timing. It depends on trust.

We believe AI should participate while the work is happening: live, multiplayer, branching, interruptible, and fast enough to keep up.

That belief shapes everything we build.

## Old Tools, New Constraints

Many of today's most important tools were not designed for AI-native collaboration.

This is not because they are bad tools. Figma, CAD systems, IDEs, project trackers, design suites, spreadsheets, cameras, DAWs, and simulation environments each grew around the constraints of their time. They assume a certain rhythm: a person edits, another person reviews, a file changes, a version is saved, a comment is left, a meeting happens, a decision is made elsewhere.

AI changes that rhythm.

When AI can generate hundreds of directions, reason across constraints, manipulate artifacts, simulate alternatives, and collaborate with multiple people at once, the old medium starts to strain. Iteration needs to become faster. Branching needs to become deeper. Context needs to be native. Models need to understand the artifact, not just text around it. Collaboration needs to happen inside the medium, not around it.

Bolting AI onto pre-AI tools will help, but it will not be enough.

We need environments where models, interfaces, and collaboration systems are designed together.

## Models And Mediums Together

Lightfast is not only an interface company, and not only a model company.

We are interested in the places where model behavior and the medium have to be designed together. Some collaboration problems can be solved with better product design. Some require new interfaces. Some require new infrastructure. Some require model-level work: interaction models, real-time feedback, multimodal understanding, controllable generation, long-context adaptation, and systems that can respond fluidly to human intent while work is unfolding.

The medium matters because it shapes what the model can understand.

A model working through a chat box sees the world differently from a model embedded in a live design environment, a codebase, a construction plan, a simulated factory floor, or a collaborative engineering session. The interface is not decoration. It is part of the intelligence of the system.

We want to build AI systems where the model and the environment make each other better.

## Starting Where The Loop Is Fastest

Our first experiments begin with software and product design.

This is where iteration loops are shortest, where digital artifacts can be changed quickly, and where the boundaries between idea, design, code, and deployment are already collapsing. It is also a domain where collaboration is dense: designers, engineers, founders, product people, and AI systems all need to work across intent, taste, implementation, review, and shipping.

But our ambition is broader than software.

The same constraints appear wherever teams create complex things: architecture, construction, manufacturing, vehicles, research labs, infrastructure, operations, and physical-world production. These domains make the collaboration problem sharper. The stakes are higher. The artifacts are richer. The number of people involved grows. The cost of slow iteration grows.

We are starting where we can learn fastest so we can build toward mediums that matter far beyond screens.

## Research By Building

Lightfast is a research and product company because we do not think these can be separated.

Research gives us the language and experiments to understand what is changing. Product forces those ideas to survive contact with real work. The best questions often appear only after a system is used by people trying to make something difficult.

We expect to publish what we learn: research notes, technical posts, prototypes, evaluations, demos, and code where useful. We want to study human-AI collaboration in the open, not as an abstract philosophy, but as a practical discipline for building better systems.

The questions we care about are concrete:

How should AI participate in real-time work with multiple people?

How should teams branch, compare, merge, and discard AI-generated directions?

How should models adapt to a team's intent, taste, constraints, and standards?

How should people interrupt, guide, correct, and collaborate with AI without leaving the flow of work?

What should a tool become when AI is not an add-on, but part of the medium itself?

## The Direction

We are building collaboration-native models and environments for complex work.

That means real-time systems. Multiplayer systems. Branching systems. Fast systems. Systems where the artifact is alive, where AI can participate directly, and where people stay meaningfully involved as capability increases.

We think the next generation of tools will feel less like static software with AI features attached, and more like living environments for thought, design, engineering, and production.

That is what Lightfast is here to build.
