# Privacy Policy

**Lightfast Pty Ltd** ABN 35 690 889 984 ("**Lightfast**", "**we**", "**us**", "**our**") operates the Lightfast platform at [lightfast.ai](https://lightfast.ai).

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information in accordance with the _Privacy Act 1988_ (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). It applies to all users of the Lightfast platform, website, and related services (the "**Service**").

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

---

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect personal information through our authentication provider, Clerk, including:

- Email address
- Full name
- Profile photograph (if provided)
- Password or single sign-on (SSO) credentials

This information is managed by Clerk and is not stored in Lightfast's own databases. We reference your account via unique identifiers only.

### 1.2 Organisation Information

When you create or join an organisation on Lightfast, we collect:

- Organisation name and identifier
- Team membership and roles
- Billing and subscription information (processed by Clerk)

### 1.3 Connected Tool Data

When you connect third-party tools (such as GitHub, Vercel, Linear, or Sentry) to Lightfast, we collect and process:

- **OAuth tokens**: Access and refresh tokens required to communicate with your connected tools. These tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM.
- **Webhook events**: Events sent from your connected tools, including pull requests, deployments, issues, errors, and other engineering activity.
- **Extracted entities**: Structured data derived from events, such as engineer names, project identifiers, endpoints, and their relationships.
- **Repository content**: If you enable repository indexing (e.g., a `.lightfast` repository), we cache specific file content to provide AI-powered context.

### 1.4 Usage and Analytics Data

We automatically collect:

- **Page views and navigation data** via PostHog, including URLs visited and anonymous usage identifiers.
- **Error and performance data** via Sentry, including error traces, browser information, and session replays (with all text masked and media blocked).
- **User agent strings** and request metadata recorded in activity audit logs.
- **IP addresses** recorded when API keys are used.

### 1.5 Information You Provide

- Feedback submitted through our in-app feedback tool (which may include screenshots).
- Communications you send to us, such as support requests or emails.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

- **Provide the Service**: Ingest events from your connected tools, build a knowledge graph of your engineering activity, and enable semantic search and AI-powered reasoning.
- **Authenticate and authorise**: Verify your identity and manage access to your organisation's data.
- **Maintain and improve**: Monitor performance, diagnose errors, and improve the Service.
- **Communicate**: Send transactional emails (e.g., invitations, notifications) via our email provider.
- **Analyse usage**: Understand how the Service is used to inform product decisions.
- **Ensure security**: Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents. Maintain audit logs of user activities.
- **Comply with law**: Meet our legal obligations, including under the _Privacy Act 1988_ (Cth).

## 5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your information, including:

- **Encryption at rest**: OAuth tokens are encrypted using AES-256-GCM. API keys are stored as SHA-256 hashes.
- **Encryption in transit**: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers uses TLS encryption.
- **Access controls**: Organisation-scoped data isolation ensures that your data is accessible only to authorised members of your organisation.
- **Session replay masking**: Sentry session replays mask all text and block all media to minimise data exposure.
- **Token scrubbing**: Authentication tokens are redacted from diagnostic breadcrumbs and logs.
- **Audit logging**: User activities are logged for security monitoring and compliance.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. If we become aware of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme.

## 6. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. Specifically:

- **Account data**: Retained while your account exists. Managed by Clerk and deleted upon account closure.
- **Organisation data**: Retained while the organisation is active. Events, entities, and related data are retained for the lifetime of the organisation.
- **OAuth tokens**: Retained while the associated tool connection is active. Deleted when a connection is revoked (cascade delete).
- **Audit logs**: Retained for compliance and security purposes.
- **Analytics data**: Retained in accordance with our analytics providers' retention policies.

You may request deletion of your personal information at any time (see Section 8).

## 7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use the following tracking technologies:

- **Analytics cookies**: Set by PostHog for product analytics. These help us understand how the Service is used.
- **Error tracking**: Sentry uses cookies and local storage for error reporting and session replay.

## 8. Your Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

- **Access**: Request access to the personal information we hold about you.
- **Correction**: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
- **Complaint**: Lodge a complaint if you believe we have breached the APPs.
- **Deletion**: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to our legal obligations.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at **support@lightfast.ai**.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the [Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)](https://www.oaic.gov.au/).
